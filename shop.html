Why the Starplanner runs from April to March
The new year feels most natural starting with the first season — spring — marking a fresh cycle in nature.
Even the Babylonians celebrated the new year around the last week of March or on April 1st, during the Akitu festival, derived from the Sumerian term Zagmuk, meaning "beginning of the year".
For me, this has a personal connection: I was born on April 1st. Starting the Starplanner on April 1st feels like coming full circle, perfectly in tune with the rhythm of the seasons and the new beginnings each spring brings.
